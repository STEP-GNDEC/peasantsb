Studying medicine in Germany generally take a bit more than six years. Firstly students are provided basic studies program with an emphasis on the basic science subjects (physics, chemistry and biology) and the fundamentals of medicine (physiology, biochemistry and anatomy). This phase ends with a state-administered medical examination. With the completion of the first phase, the student moves on to the main course of study in core areas of medicine that includes lectures, seminars and laboratory works. This again is followed by a year of practical training period in a clinic or hospital, which is known as the “Practical year”, which focuses on surgery, medicine and a self chosen subject by the student. This ends with the second medical exam and followed by training to become a specialist having an md in Germany.
