Living In Germany

After you have gone through the administrative bad dream of getting your permanent residence, working permit, or citizensh ip, you are prepared to investigate what’s truly going on with Germany. Obviously, you have heard that this is the European place where there are valuable open doors with wonderful urban communities and towns, and it’s all so extremely spotless. However, how is life in Germany truly? Here we will carry on wit h a couple of qualities of life in Germany, the best and least expensive spots to live in Germany, j ust as the average cost for basic items.

Currency

The Euro is the official currency of the Germany. Perhaps you have known this, but if you have come from a country where the currency is different it is advised that you will need to exchange it for Euros or you won’t able to buy anything. The other option is that y ou can use debit or credit card which will be charged with the expenses.

The Language

Germans communicate in German. Obviously, they communicate in different languages like English, however, to get around the country, it is ideal to at minimum have a few essential language abilities. You may be disapproved if you have no clue about how to say greetings, please, and thank you in German.

Peaceful Country

Life is very much occupied in Germany as in some other spot. In any case, overall there is harmony and calm anyplace you go. Aside from the clubs which will rave with techno music. Other than that, you will observe that Germans love their quiet, that there is low corruption and that it will not be hard to get a job.

Workers’ Rights

Employees are high valued in Germany. In Germany there are many organizations that protect their rights and if you complain someone will definitely take action. As an employee, you will not be exploited and are not at the mercy of mean boss.

Public Transport

The public transports are on time in Germany. Whether it is a bus or train. They are also very clean. Public Transports are widely used in Germany. It is an amazing experience. If you’d say that you were late due to bus or a train this excuse will not acceptable in Germany. Orderliness and Timeliness are extremely important in Germany.

Cities that are built for bikes

The roads are in Germany built for all kinds of transport. They are pedestrian friendly and almost bicycle friendly. Almost all roads have a specially designed lane for bikes and you will see cyclists everywhere.

Recycling

The trash management system in Germany is very good. You’ll see special bins for all kind of garbage and there is specific time. Germany is environment friendly country. It will involve most environmental activities.

Travelling Opportunities

Germany is in the middle of Europe and travelling in Europe is much cheaper than other places, especially from the US. You can be in another country in a few hours. It gives you immense opportunities to travel even within the country as it is quite big, and outside of it too, to see different cultures.

Food

People go to Germany with expectation of eating a lot of sausage and nothing else. Germany has wide selection of food, from the meat to the desserts. You get a huge range of breads and other baked goods as well. You might not be able to find the comfort foods of your own country easily, but you will have the opportunity to try many different dishes and ingredients that will delicious.

The studies in Germany are a mixture of practical and theoretical aspects of education. The lectures are not only taught theoretically to the students, but they also get an opportunity to perform practicals related to their respective fields of study. At the end of every term, the students get grades for their final examination and for speeches, assignments and practical projects. Depending on the subject, the composition of these parts can differ. Practical education can also be offered through internships, which are an obligation in some subjects. For some weeks or months the students have to work in a company and use their theoretical knowledge in real life situations to gain experience. This is also a good chance to find a job for the working life after university. The graduate degrees from university are accepted and estimated worldwide. The first graduate degree can be obtained after six to eight terms and is called Bachelor.

Afterwards it is followed by a Masters degree after another two to four terms. It is mandatory to pass the exams for both the degrees and also a specific graduate thesis is required. For subjects such as medicine, dentistry, law and pharmaceutics, teaching degree and another degree is required, which is called Staatsexamen. After the Masters degree, students can also do their PhD to get their Doctoral degree. Academic education in Germany not only gives firm and basic knowledge, but also theoretical background and practical application about a particular field of study. After successful completion of studies an alumni becomes much able to succeed in his/her working environment.
